## 💡 Pro Tips for Recording

### **Screen Recording Best Practices**

**Before Recording**:
1. Close unnecessary applications (reduce CPU load)
2. Clear browser cache and cookies
3. Disable browser extensions (except essential ones)
4. Turn off notifications (Windows, macOS, browser)
5. Prepare a clean desktop background
6. Test recording 30 seconds to verify quality
7. Have script visible on second monitor

**During Recording**:
1. **Cursor Movement**: Slow and deliberate
2. **Clicks**: Pause briefly before and after clicks
3. **Scrolling**: Smooth, not too fast
4. **Form Filling**: Natural typing pace
5. **Navigation**: Allow pages to fully load
6. **Hover Effects**: Hold hover for 1-2 seconds to show effect
7. **Transitions**: Pause 1 second between major actions

**Monitoring Page Specific**:
1. Use real or realistic demo camera feeds (not placeholder images)
2. Ensure camera feeds show actual construction activity
3. Demonstrate live nature with timestamps
4. Show multiple cameras simultaneously
5. Include drone footage that's visually impressive
6. Demonstrate alert system with realistic scenario
7. Highlight role-based access clearly

---

### **Audio Recording Best Practices**

**Recording Environment**:
- Quiet room with minimal echo
- Use soft furnishings to dampen sound
- Close windows and doors
- Turn off fans, AC, refrigerators
- Record during quiet hours

**Microphone Technique**:
- Position mic 6-8 inches from mouth
- Use pop filter to reduce plosives (p, b, t sounds)
- Maintain consistent distance
- Speak at 45-degree angle to mic (not directly into it)

**Voice Delivery**:
- Warm up voice before recording (vocal exercises)
- Stay hydrated (water, not cold)
- Smile while talking (sounds more engaging)
- Stand while recording (better breath support)
- Take breaks to avoid voice fatigue
- Record multiple takes for best performance

**Post-Processing**:
- Noise reduction (-30 to -40dB)
- EQ for vocal clarity (boost 2-5kHz range)
- Compression for consistent volume
- De-essing to reduce harsh "s" sounds
- Normalization to -3dB peak
