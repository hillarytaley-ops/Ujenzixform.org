## 🎯 **VOICEOVER TIPS**

### **General Guidelines:**
- Speak clearly and professionally with Kenyan English accent
- Smile while recording - it shows in your voice
- Use natural pauses for emphasis (marked by periods)
- Build energy gradually from opening to closing
- Pronounce "Kujenga Pamoja" with authentic Swahili pronunciation
- When listing features, use slight vocal inflection to differentiate items
- Let the excitement about the platform show through authentically

### **Key Pronunciation:**
- **MradiPro:** Mrah-dee-Pro (emphasis on "Mrah")
- **Kujenga Pamoja:** Koo-jen-gah Pah-mo-jah
- **Nairobi:** Nye-row-bee
- **Mombasa:** Mom-bah-sah
- **Eldoret:** El-doh-ret
- **Kisumu:** Kee-soo-moo

### **Breathing Points:**
Natural places to take breaths are marked by periods. Don't rush through sentences - let the message breathe and land with the audience.

## 📊 **RECORDING SPECIFICATIONS**

### **Technical Requirements:**
- **Sample Rate:** 48kHz minimum
- **Bit Depth:** 24-bit
- **Format:** WAV (uncompressed)
- **Microphone:** Professional condenser mic
- **Environment:** Quiet, treated room (no echo)
- **Pop Filter:** Essential for clean plosives
- **Processing:** Light compression and de-essing

### **Delivery Format:**
- Full take (3:30 complete)
- Section breaks included
- Alternative takes for key sections
- Raw and lightly processed versions
